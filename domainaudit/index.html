Domain Audit

Bulk-check SPF, DKIM, DMARC, MX, and registrar across a list of domains. Spots missing or misconfigured email authentication, identifies the primary mail provider, and lists every additional sender authorized through SPF includes.

What each check means

SPF — The TXT record on the apex starting with v=spf1. Lists which servers can send mail as the domain. Multiple SPF records is invalid (they must merge). Watch for the RFC 7208 10-DNS-lookup limit on include:/a:/mx: mechanisms.

DKIM — Public key(s) at <selector>._domainkey.<domain>. Selectors are arbitrary per provider, so this tool can only probe common selectors. Not detected means no DKIM at the selectors we tried — the domain may still have DKIM under a custom selector you'd need to specify in Advanced.

DMARC — The TXT record on _dmarc.<domain>. Sets policy for what receivers do when SPF/DKIM fail: p=none (monitoring only), p=quarantine, p=reject. Without DMARC, spoofing protection is partial at best.

Mail provider — Inferred from the highest-priority MX record. Tells you who handles inbound mail (Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, Proofpoint, etc.).

Authorized senders — Every include: in the SPF chain, mapped to the friendly name of the service that owns it (Mailchimp, SendGrid, etc.). Use this to spot legacy senders that should be removed, or to confirm every expected service is listed.

Reports — Whether DMARC has a rua= address. Without DMARC reporting, you have no visibility into which services are trying to send as the domain. Reports are how you discover senders that need to be added to (or removed from) SPF.

Registrar — Pulled via RDAP (the modern WHOIS replacement) from rdap.org. Some country-code TLDs return limited info; we surface what's available.
