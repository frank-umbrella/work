SPF Toolkit

Look up an existing record, build a new one for the most common providers, then validate the result. SPF records list which mail servers are allowed to send email for a domain — receivers reject (or quarantine) anything that doesn't match.

1. Look up SPF records on a domain

Resolves the domain's TXT records over DNS-over-HTTPS (Google Public DNS) and highlights anything starting with v=spf1.

Tip: drop in any domain you receive mail for (e.g. your own, a vendor, or a sender you suspect is being spoofed).

2. Generate a new SPF record

Pick the senders that are authorized for the domain. The record updates as you tick boxes — copy the result into a single TXT record at the apex of your zone.

How to publish this
  • One SPF record per domain. If a record already exists, merge the new mechanisms into it — don't add a second TXT.
  • The TXT record sits at the zone apex (i.e. the host is @ or your domain itself, not spf.example.com).
  • Cap a single record at 10 DNS-lookup mechanisms (include, a, mx, ptr, exists, redirect). The validator below counts them recursively.
  • A single TXT string is limited to 255 chars; longer records must be split into multiple quoted strings (most DNS UIs do this for you).

3. SPF Validator & raw checker

Paste any SPF string. The validator parses every mechanism, recursively follows include: and redirect=, and counts the total DNS lookups against the RFC 7208 limit of 10.

SPF Toolkit · runs entirely in your browser; DNS lookups go to Google Public DNS over HTTPS. No data is sent anywhere else.
